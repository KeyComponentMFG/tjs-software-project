## ARTICLE VI — STRIKE SYSTEM & QUALITY ACCOUNTABILITY

6.1. **Strike Events.** The following events each constitute one (1) strike against the responsible Member:

   (a) Customer message not responded to within 30 minutes during business hours (per occurrence)
   (b) Order not shipped within 2 business days (per order)
   (c) Defective product shipped resulting in a return or complaint (per incident)
   (d) Negative review (1-3 stars) caused by the Member's fulfilled order (per review)
   (e) Failed print not reported or tracked (per incident)
   (f) Taking an owner draw without Managing Member approval (per occurrence)
   (g) Missing a monthly contribution review without 48-hour advance notice
   (h) Not restocking inventory when assigned and stock reaches low levels
   (i) Shipping wrong item, wrong quantity, or to wrong address (per incident)
   (j) Using incorrect print settings after being informed of the correct settings (per incident)
   (k) Not responding to partner messages for 5+ consecutive hours
   (l) Not completing a requested task within 18 hours
   (m) Inaccurate inventory reporting leading to fulfillment issues
   (n) Not using the Company dashboard for 7+ consecutive days
   (o) Using excessive material/filament without addressing waste
   (p) Not sharing supplier information or better pricing when discovered
   (q) Running out of filament/material mid-print and not notifying partner immediately
   (r) Actions that cause loss of Etsy Star Seller status (late shipments, slow responses, low reviews)
   (s) Printing with incorrect or unsafe filament/material
   (t) Actions that trigger an Etsy payment hold or account warning
   (u) Not maintaining up-to-date STL files and print profiles on the shared OneDrive

6.2. **Strike Severity.**

   (a) **Standard Strike (1 point):** Late response, late shipment, missed restock, late task completion, dashboard non-use
   (b) **Major Strike (2 points):** Defective product shipped, negative review caused, unapproved draw, inaccurate inventory, excessive waste, actions causing loss of Star Seller status, triggering Etsy payment hold, not maintaining shared OneDrive files
   (c) **Critical Strike (3 points):** Repeated defective shipments after notification, knowingly shipping defective products, printing with unsafe/incorrect materials, customer case opened due to negligence, password changes without notification, restricting partner access, product safety incident, sabotage or retaliation

6.3. **Consequences by Strike Points (rolling 90-day window):**

   | Strike Points | Consequence |
   |---------------|-------------|
   | 3 points | Written warning with documented improvement plan |
   | 5 points | Owner draws suspended until points drop below 3 |
   | 8 points | Formal contribution review — ownership adjustment discussion |
   | 12 points | Automatic ownership reduction per Article X, Section 10.4 |
   | 15+ points | Forced buyout territory per Article X, Section 10.6 |

6.4. **Strike Reset.** Strike points expire after 90 days of clean performance (no new strikes). Points do NOT reset if new strikes occur within the 90-day window — the clock restarts with each new strike.

6.5. **Financial Responsibility for Quality Failures.**

   (a) **Refunds caused by poor quality, late shipping, or wrong items:** The responsible Member's capital account is debited for: outbound shipping label + return shipping label + any Etsy fees not refunded. The refund amount is a shared business loss.
   (b) **Negative reviews:** A pattern of negative reviews (3+ in any 90-day period) from one Member's fulfilled orders constitutes a Major Strike for each review beyond the second. Reviews are a backbone of the business — bad reviews set you back exponentially and make recovery harder.
   (c) **Inventory waste:** Products damaged through improper storage, handling, incorrect print settings, or negligence are debited from the responsible Member's capital account at material cost.
   (d) **Missed customer messages resulting in cases or lost sales:** Standard Strike per incident.

6.6. **Strike Recording Procedure.** All strikes shall be formally recorded as follows:

   (a) The Member issuing the strike shall send Written Notice to the other Member within 72 hours of the event, specifying: (i) the date and time of the event, (ii) which strike category applies (Section 6.1), (iii) the severity level (Section 6.2), and (iv) supporting evidence (dashboard data, screenshots, timestamps, or customer communications).
   (b) The strike shall be logged in the Company Dashboard with the above information.
   (c) The receiving Member has 48 hours to dispute the strike in writing. If no dispute is filed within 48 hours, the strike is deemed accepted.
   (d) Both Members shall have access to the full strike log at all times.

6.7. **Strike Dispute Resolution.** If a Member disputes a strike within the 48-hour window:

   (a) Both Members shall review the relevant dashboard data together within 5 days.
   (b) If the data clearly supports the strike (timestamps, tracking numbers, customer messages), the strike stands regardless of the dispute.
   (c) If the data is ambiguous, both Members shall attempt to reach agreement within 7 days.
   (d) If no agreement is reached, the Managing Member's determination stands. The disputing Member may escalate to mediation under Article XIII, but the strike remains in effect during the mediation process.
   (e) If mediation overturns the strike, it is removed retroactively and any consequences triggered by that strike are reversed.

6.8. **Cumulative Effect.** Strike Points are cumulative within the 90-day window. Multiple strikes on the same day each count separately. There is no daily cap on strikes. If a Member ships 5 defective products on one day, that is 5 separate Major Strikes (10 points).

## ARTICLE X — FAILURE TO CONTRIBUTE / REMEDIES

10.1. **Written Notice.** If a Member fails to meet the contribution standard in Article IV, accumulates strike points per Article VI, or violates communication requirements per Article V, the Contributing Member shall provide written notice (email or text is sufficient) specifying the deficiency with supporting dashboard data.

10.2. **Cure Period.** The non-contributing Member has **14 days** from receipt of notice to cure the deficiency and demonstrate sustained return to equal contribution.

10.3. **Immediate Consequences (upon written notice):**

   (a) Non-contributing Member's owner draws are **immediately suspended** until contribution is restored and maintained for 30 consecutive days
   (b) Non-contributing Member loses voting rights on non-essential business decisions
   (c) Managing Member gains sole operational authority during the deficiency period

10.4. **Ownership Adjustment Procedure.** If the Contribution Deficiency is not cured within the 14-day Cure Period, the following formal process applies:

   (a) **Calculation.** The Managing Member shall prepare a Contribution Report using Company Dashboard data covering the preceding 90 days. The report shall include: orders fulfilled per Member, customer response metrics, refund rates, products designed, shipping timeliness, Strike Points accumulated, and any other Contribution Metrics tracked by the Dashboard.
   (b) **Proposed Adjustment.** Based on the Contribution Report, the Managing Member shall propose an ownership adjustment reflecting actual contribution ratios. Example: If the Dashboard shows Member A performed 75% of measurable work and Member B performed 25%, the proposed adjustment is 75/25.
   (c) **Notice.** The Managing Member shall deliver Written Notice of the proposed adjustment to the other Member, attaching the Contribution Report and proposed new Ownership Percentages.
   (d) **Response Period.** The other Member has 7 days to respond in writing. They may: (i) accept the adjustment, (ii) dispute specific data points with evidence, or (iii) fail to respond, in which case the adjustment is deemed accepted.
   (e) **Resolution of Disputes.** If specific data points are disputed, both Members shall review the raw Dashboard data together. If no agreement is reached within 14 days of the initial notice, the Managing Member's proposed adjustment takes effect, subject to the other Member's right to mediation under Article XIII.
   (f) **Effective Date.** The ownership adjustment takes effect on the date of acceptance, deemed acceptance (no response), or Managing Member's determination after the 14-day dispute period.
   (g) **Documentation.** The adjustment shall be documented in a written amendment signed by both Members (or by the Managing Member alone if the other Member refuses to sign after deemed acceptance). A copy shall be stored in the Company Dashboard.
   (h) **Cumulative.** Ownership adjustments are cumulative and do not reset unless both Members unanimously agree in writing.
   (i) **Profit Distributions.** All profit distributions and draws adjust immediately to match the new Ownership Percentages as of the effective date.
   (j) **Principle.** Ownership in this Company has value only when backed by active, consistent, equal contribution. Ownership without contribution is not recognized.

10.5. **Buyout Right (Contributing Member's option).** If a Member's ownership drops below 30%:

   (a) The Contributing Member may offer to buy out the remaining interest at **fair market value minus documented damages**
   (b) Damages include: all refund costs attributed to the non-contributing Member, excess draws, capital account debits, and labor costs incurred covering their duties (valued at $25/hour)
   (c) Buyout payable over 6 months at the Contributing Member's discretion

10.6. **Forced Buyout.** If a Member's ownership drops below 20%, OR their contribution falls below 15% for any 60-day period, OR they accumulate 15+ strike points:

   (a) The Contributing Member may initiate a **forced buyout at 60% of fair market value**
   (b) Payable over 12 months
   (c) The departing Member must transfer all store access, account credentials, Company equipment, and Company IP within 14 days
   (d) Non-compete provisions in Article XIV activate immediately

      - (iv) Add the Company's current cash on hand (bank balance plus Etsy Payments balance).
      - (v) Subtract all Company liabilities (credit card balances, outstanding debts).
      - (vi) The resulting figure is the FMV.

Both Members agree these damages would be uncertain and difficult to prove precisely (per Oklahoma liquidated damages standards under 15 O.S. § 215), making this liquidated amount reasonable.

The steep reduction reflects that retaliation (sabotage, data deletion, customer interference) causes catastrophic, hard-to-quantify harm to a small e-commerce business where reputation and data are the primary assets.

Each Member provides printing capacity at their location. Both Members are expected to contribute to all other aspects of the business equally — customer service, design, administration, and operations.

### Contribution & Effort

**Q14: What happens to designs if a Member departs?**
All product designs are Company IP per Article IX. A departing Member may not use, copy, or create derivatives of any Company designs.

**Q15: How are print jobs distributed?**
Printing is split based on print time, available inventory, and materials at each location to maximize efficiency and timely fulfillment.

**Q16: What is the minimum customer response time?**
30 minutes during Business Hours (9 AM – 9 PM, 7 days/week). This is a core commitment that directly impacts the Company's Etsy ranking and reputation.

**Q17: What are the quality expectations?**
Both Members must continuously improve print quality, reduce print times, and optimize material usage. Shipping a product with a known defect instead of fixing the issue first is a Critical Strike.

### Refunds & Quality

**Q18: Who is responsible for a bad review?**
The Member who printed and shipped the order is accountable. Bad reviews severely impact search ranking and sales velocity — consequences are defined in Article VI.

**Q19: Are failed prints tracked?**
Yes. Material waste and failed prints are tracked and attributed to the responsible Member per Section 6.5.

### Finances

**Q20: How do owner draws work?**
Equal draws at equal times, approved by the Managing Member. Neither Member may take more than the other per Section 8.2.

### Strike System

**Q22: What are the strike thresholds?**
3 points = written warning; 5 = draws suspended; 8 = formal review; 12 = ownership adjustment; 15+ = forced buyout. Points reset after 90 days of clean performance.

**Q23: What constitutes a strike?**
All strike events are defined in Section 6.1, including late responses, late shipments, quality failures, unapproved draws, inventory misreporting, and communication failures.

**Q24: What are Business Hours?**
9:00 AM to 9:00 PM in each Member's local time zone, 7 days per week.

### Team Expansion

**Q25: Can we hire employees or bring in a third partner?**
Both require unanimous consent per Section 7.2.

**Q26: Is this a full-time or part-time commitment?**
The Company is operated as a side business. However, both Members are held to equal contribution standards regardless of outside employment. Having other jobs does not excuse reduced effort.

### Social Media & Marketing

**Q27: Who owns social media accounts?**
All social media accounts are Company property per Article XII. If a Member creates an account but fails to actively maintain it within 60 days, the Managing Member may take over administration.

### Suppliers & Purchasing

**Q28: Who is responsible for supplier relationships?**
Both Members share this responsibility. If a supplier relationship fails due to one Member's negligence, that Member bears the cost impact per Section 4.4.

**Q29: What are the purchasing rules?**
Previously purchased items may be reordered freely. New items require communication first — 5-hour response window applies. Unauthorized new purchases are the purchasing Member's personal expense per Section 11.3.

### Personal Use

**Q30: Can Members use Company printers for personal projects?**
Yes, provided they use their own materials, do not interfere with Company production, and do not use Company inventory or products. Exception for product photography with Managing Member approval per Section 11.5.

### Communication

**Q31: Are there minimum weekly hours?**
No fixed hourly minimum. Contribution is measured by output and metrics, not hours logged. If contribution is unequal, the provisions of Article X apply.

**Q32: What happens to inactive social media accounts?**
If a Member creates an account but doesn't maintain it within 60 days, the Managing Member takes administrative control per Section 12.2.

### Shipping & Returns

**Q33: Who pays for inter-location shipping?**
The Company pays for regular order fulfillment shipping. For planned absences over 7 days, the absent Member covers material transfer costs personally per Section 11.2(e).

**Q34: Where are customer returns sent?**
All returns go to the Company address (2725 E 47th St, Tulsa, OK 74105) for centralized quality inspection per Section 11.1.

### Listing Management

**Q35: How are listing decisions made?**
Data-driven analysis of sales volume, margin, refund rate, print time, and demand. The Managing Member resolves disagreements. No listing may be removed simply because a Member doesn't want to print it per Section 11.6.

### Absence Coverage

**Q36: What is the absence policy?**
7-day advance notice, pre-print inventory, hand off pending orders, maintain customer service remotely, adjust listings if necessary. Maximum 14 consecutive days without mutual consent per Section 11.2.

### Suppliers & Cost Optimization

**Q40: What if a Member finds a better supplier?**
Both Members are required to share supplier information immediately. Both locations should use identical materials for product consistency per Section 4.4.

**Q41: Who purchases packaging supplies?**
Company expense. Both Members are responsible for keeping their location's packaging supplies stocked and quantities communicated.

### Store Suspension

**Q42: What if a Member's actions cause an Etsy store suspension?**
If the suspension results from negligence or failure to meet standards (not an honest accident), severe consequences apply including potential ownership forfeiture.

### Tax & Records

**Q43: Who handles tax and financial records?**
The Managing Member currently handles all administrative and tax compliance work. Both Members are expected to contribute to record-keeping, especially as Company tools become more streamlined.

### Investment Decisions

**Q44: How are reinvestment decisions handled?**
The Managing Member decides on reinvestment strategy. If a Member agrees to support an investment direction but fails to match the commitment, their ownership may be adjusted accordingly.

### Customer Data & Software

**Q45: Can either Member use Company data for personal purposes?**
No. Neither Member may use customer data, business analytics, or the other Member's software for personal or competing purposes. Each Member's personally-built software remains their property per Section 9.2. This applies equally to both Members.

### Dashboard Usage

**Q46: Is dashboard usage required?**
Yes. Both Members must log in weekly at minimum, upload relevant data, and review metrics. Failure to use the dashboard is a Standard Strike.

### Equipment Repairs

**Q47: Who pays for printer repairs?**
If the damage was caused by negligence, the responsible Member pays. Normal wear and tear is a Company expense.

### Inventory & Materials

**Q48: How is filament and material purchasing handled?**
Both locations purchase identical materials through the Company account to maintain product consistency across locations.

### Exit Terms

**Q50: What happens if a Member voluntarily leaves?**
Voluntary departure is presumed to be forfeiture per Section 10.7. A Member who has maintained equal contribution in good standing may request a buyout at Fair Market Value, subject to the Managing Member's consideration.

### Peak Season

**Q51: How is peak season handled?**
Both Members must agree on a seasonal preparation plan at least 60 days in advance. During peak periods, both Members are expected to be fully available.

### Injury & Disability

**Q52: How are injuries or health issues handled?**
Hospitalization or genuine physical incapacitation is a valid Force Majeure Event. However, the nature of this business allows most tasks to be performed from a phone or computer. Contribution remains the primary standard.

**Q53: When does ownership have value?**
Ownership has value only when backed by active, consistent, equal contribution over a sustained period. It is earned through effort and persistence, not by simply being named on paper.

### Print Prioritization

**Q54: How are print jobs prioritized?**
Orders with committed delivery dates take priority. Longer print times should be started first to prevent late shipments. Beyond that, each Member manages their own queue as long as everything ships on time.

### Communication Standards

**Q55: What are the partner communication requirements?**
Both Members must be reachable via text and respond within 8 hours. Quick task requests must be completed within 18 hours. Failure to respond to 5 consecutive hours of messages results in lost contribution credit for that day. Consistent communication is the foundation of this partnership.

### Account Control

**Q56: How are Company accounts and credentials managed?**
The Managing Member maintains a master record of all credentials. Verification codes and recovery emails are routed through the Managing Member's contact information. Both Members have operational access to store dashboards for fulfillment.

### Quality Standards

**Q57: Is there a separate quality standards document?**
Yes. A Company Operations Manual maintained by the Managing Member, updatable with 7 days written notice without amending this Agreement, per Section 11.10.

### Customer Contacts

**Q58: How are customer contacts through personal channels handled?**
All customer interactions must go through Company channels. Exception: handwritten notes or personal touches included with shipped products.

### Side Businesses

**Q59: Can Members operate unrelated businesses?**
Yes. Members may pursue unrelated business interests provided their Company contribution remains equal per Article IV. Contribution standards do not change based on outside commitments.

### Account Security

**Q60: What happens if credentials are changed without notice?**
Changing passwords or access credentials without notifying the other Member is a Critical Strike per Section 7.6(f). If combined with refusal to communicate, it constitutes a complete breach as it prevents the other Member from conducting Company business.

Both Members agree to split Company debt, loans, and credit obligations evenly. Debt incurred without the other Member's consent is the sole responsibility of the Member who incurred it per Section 3.8.

Communication, equal contribution, quality standards, and mutual accountability. Every provision in this Agreement flows from these four principles.

Quarterly sit-downs assess contribution equality and agreement compliance. Annual review discusses potential amendments per Sections 4.7 and 19.20.
